summary

Medical professionalism

is a set of values, behaviors and relationships that strengthen public trust in doctors

  • Is based on?
    • Altruism

      best interest of the pt

    • accountability

      required at : individual pts, societal and pro level

    • excellence

      involves a reliable effort to exceed normal expectations and make a commitment to life-long learning.

    • humanism
  • major components of professionalism?
    • medical ethics

      refers to guidelines and behaviors expected from a medical pro with moral integrity to exhibit

      • components of medical ethics?
        • pt-physician relationship
          • core element?

            clear and effective communication

            • how?
              • listen to pt
              • share info with pt
              • answer questions
              • makes sure pt is well informed
            • importance of effective communication?
              • accurate DDX
              • better pt compliance
              • contribute to dr competence and self assurance
              • contribute to pt satisfaction
              • contribute to cost and resource effectiveness
          • dealing with difficult pt?
            • examples
              • entitled demanders

                frequent fliers, tests lovers

              • manipulative help-rejecter

                doc!, it ain't working

              • know-it-all
              • uncooperative
            • how

              just remember

              • It's not personal
              • start with a good first impression
              • help pt gain emotional control ( give them time and space when needed)
          • dealing with pt family?

            remember that confidentiality is to the pt, ask pt if it's okay for them to stay

          • breaking bad news?

            SPIKES model

            • Setting up the interview
              • private place, adequate time, suggest getting supportive company.
              • have all data available, intro yourself
            • Perception of the patient

              determine what the pt knows and what's wanted to know

            • Invitation by the patient
              • most pt wants to know it all
              • discuss information disclosure ( should the family know)
              • if pt don't want details, involve relatives
            • Knowledge to the patient
              • warn first
              • avoid jargons
              • avoid false reassurance
            • Emotions of the patient

              respond to pt emo with empathy

            • Strategy and summary
              • ask if pt is ready to discuss first
              • explore pt ICE ( ideas, concerns and expectations)
              • understand pt's goals ( like controlling a symptom)
              • give reasonable hope of what's possible to accomplish
          • counseling

            is a professional relationship and activity in which one person attempt to help another to understand and to solve his or her adjustment problems

            • WHO def?

              well-focused process, limited in time and specific, which uses the interaction to help people deal with their problems and respond in a proper way to specific difficulties in order to develop new coping strategies.

            • med def?

              valid intervention made of a quality interaction between the counselor (who can be the doctor) and the patient with The main purpose of helping the patient amplify the vision of the specific situation and discover the proper potential in dealing with the conditions of disease, in order to promote a better quality of life.

        • physician-physician relationship
        • healthcare-system-physician relationship
        • society-physician relationship

    • communication skills

      is a set of skills enabling a person to convey info so that it's received and understood and achieve its intended effect

      • types of communication?

        verbal & non-verbal

      • categories of communication?
        • intrapersonal

          self-talk or thinking

        • direct interpersonal
          • why strong feedback component?

            cuz of its immediacy and primacy (loci)

          • four principles of interpersonal communication?
            • inescapable
            • irreversible
            • complicated
            • contextual
        • small group
        • organizational
        • mass
        • public
      • barriers to communication?

        anything that leads to loss of meaning while conveying a message

        • include?

          lang, emo, stress, ignorance.

          basically: not listening, not being present, not understanding

        • written

      means critical thinking and problem solving, ethical and responsible role fulfillment ,initiative and accountability and appropriate nonverbal gestures and good performance.

        • Professional Health Care
          • main components?
            • Risk Management

              an organized effort to identify, assess, and reduce, where appropriate, risk to patients, visitors, staff and organizational assets.

                1. Identify risks
                1. Analyze risks
                1. Evaluate risks
                1. Treat/Manage Risks
